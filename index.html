These are letters that my great-grandfather Aron wrote to my great-grandmother Manya in the months before the Nazi invasion of Kiev.

Manya had evacuated with my grandfather, who was then six years old, to Ulyanovsk (about a thousand miles away). Aron had been mobilized into the Soviet army. Aron was captured shortly after the last letter was written.

After the war, my family learned that all the prisoners were checked for circumcision and every Jew, including Aron, was immediately executed. These letters arrived after the war.

Transcription and translation by Stella Sokolovskaya and Arkady Rubinsteyn from the Evidence of the Holocaust project in Chicago.
